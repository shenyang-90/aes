**Status:** Open

## Summary

AES-192 and AES-256 key length support is incomplete. The RTL appears to only process 128-bit keys regardless of KEY_LEN register setting.

## Error Details

### Test Failure Log
```

  Actual:   00112233445566778898a8b8c8d8e8f9

[Test 0] AES-256-ECB-0
[FAIL] AES-256-ECB
  Expected: 8ea2b7ca516745bfeafc49904b496089
  Actual:   00112233445566778899aabbccddeefe
```

### Root Cause Analysis

1. **Key Schedule**: `key_schedule.v` only implements AES-128 key expansion (4-word key)
   - AES-192 requires 6-word key (52 subkeys)
   - AES-256 requires 8-word key (60 subkeys)

2. **Round Count**: Core always uses 10 rounds regardless of key length
   - AES-128: 10 rounds ✅
   - AES-192: 12 rounds ❌
   - AES-256: 14 rounds ❌

## Expected Behavior

| Key Length | Key Words | Round Keys | Rounds | NIST Standard |
|------------|-----------|------------|--------|---------------|
| AES-128 | 4 | 44 | 10 | ✅ FIPS-197 |
| AES-192 | 6 | 52 | 12 | ❌ Not implemented |
| AES-256 | 8 | 60 | 14 | ❌ Not implemented |
